## Sample Scene (Filled Example)

```
INT. DIVE BAR - NIGHT

Neon beer signs flicker. A HAND wipes a glass. This is 
MICKEY (50s), bartender and former trainer, eyes like 
broken glass.

The door opens. Wind blows in a YOUNG WOMAN (22), 
hood up, gloved hands clenched.

MICKEY
We're closed.

YOUNG WOMAN
I need to learn.

MICKEY
Learn what?

YOUNG WOMAN
How to hit back.

Mickey stops wiping. Looks at her hands. The gloves 
hide something — scars, maybe. Or worse.

MICKEY
You got a name?

YOUNG WOMAN
The name doesn't matter.

She pulls off a glove. Reveals bruised knuckles. 
Fresh. Maybe hours old.

MICKEY
(quietly)
Who did that to you?

YOUNG WOMAN
Who do you think?

She meets his eyes. Mickey sees something he recognizes. 
Not fear. Something harder.

MICKEY
Lesson one. You never take the glove off 
in my bar unless you're ready to use 
what's underneath.

YOUNG WOMAN
I'm ready.

Mickey sets down the glass. Studies her.

MICKEY
Lesson two. Everybody says they're ready. 
Nobody is.

He reaches under the bar. Pulls out a jump rope.

MICKEY (CONT'D)
Show me fifty. Then we'll talk about 
hitting.

She takes the rope. Starts jumping. Rhythm is terrible. 
But she doesn't stop.

Mickey watches. Something shifts in his face. Not hope. 
Something more dangerous.

FADE TO:
```

## Formatting Rules

### Scene Headings (Sluglines)
```
INT. COFFEE SHOP - DAY
EXT. CITY STREET - NIGHT
INT. HOUSE - LIVING ROOM - CONTINUOUS
```

### Action Lines
- Present tense, active voice
- 3-4 lines maximum per paragraph
- Character names in CAPS on first appearance
- Important sounds in CAPS: `BANG. The window SHATTERS.`

### Dialogue
```
CHARACTER NAME
(parenthetical direction)
Dialogue text here.
```

### Transitions (Use Sparingly)
- `CUT TO:` — standard (usually implied)
- `FADE TO:` — passage of time
- `DISSOLVE TO:` — gentle transition
- `SMASH CUT TO:` — jarring contrast

---

## Dialogue Checklist

- [ ] Each character has a distinct voice
- [ ] Dialogue sounds natural when read aloud
- [ ] Subtext carries meaning beyond the words
- [ ] No on-the-nose exposition ("As you know, Bob...")
- [ ] Action lines reveal emotion, not dialogue tags
- [ ] Pauses and silences are used as punctuation

---

## Revision Notes

**After first draft:**
- [ ] Read all dialogue aloud
- [ ] Check that each scene has a purpose and a turn
- [ ] Verify visual storytelling (could a deaf person follow the story?)
- [ ] Trim 10% — screenplays improve with compression
- [ ] Ensure protagonist drives the action in Act III
