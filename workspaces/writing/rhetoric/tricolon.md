# Tricolon
## Rule of Three

---

## Definition

**Tricolon** is a rhetorical device that uses a series of three parallel words, phrases, or clauses. The power of three creates rhythm, emphasis, and memorability.

---

## Purpose

- Create rhythmic, memorable phrases
- Build emphasis through repetition
- Provide completeness (beginning, middle, end)
- Make ideas stick in reader's mind
- Add rhetorical power to arguments

---

## When to Use

- **Speeches**: "Government of the people, by the people, for the people"
- **Slogans**: "Faster, higher, stronger"
- **Persuasive writing**: Building to a climax
- **Branding**: "Easy, fast, affordable"
- **Emphasis**: When you want something to resonate

---

## Types of Tricolon

### Ascending Tricolon
Build intensity with each element:
- "I came, I saw, I conquered"
- "Small, medium, large"
- "Good, better, best"

### Descending Tricolon
Decrease intensity:
- "The world, the nation, the individual"
- "Life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness"

### Isocolon (Equal Length)
Three phrases of similar length:
- "Never in the past, never in the present, never in the future"

---

## Examples

### Classic Examples
✅ "Life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness" (Declaration of Independence)
✅ "Blood, sweat, and tears" (Churchill)
✅ "Government of the people, by the people, for the people" (Lincoln)
✅ "Veni, vidi, vici" / "I came, I saw, I conquered" (Caesar)

### Modern Examples
✅ "Fast, reliable, and secure" (tech writing)
✅ "Learn it, practice it, master it" (educational)
✅ "Plan, execute, succeed" (business)

---

## How to Build a Tricolon

1. **Choose your concept**
2. **Find three related elements**
3. **Make them parallel in structure**
4. **Optional: Build intensity**

### Example Construction
Concept: Describe a product
- Step 1: "It's affordable"
- Step 2: Add second: "It's affordable and reliable"
- Step 3: Complete the three: "It's affordable, reliable, and innovative"

---

## Common Patterns

### Adjectives
"The app is fast, beautiful, and intuitive."

### Verbs
"We design, we build, we deliver."

### Nouns
"Time, money, and effort—all wasted."

### Phrases
"We will fight on the beaches, we will fight on the landing grounds, we will fight in the fields."

---

## Application Note

Tricolon is one of the most powerful and accessible rhetorical devices. Use it when you want something to be remembered. The rule of three is psychologically satisfying—two feels incomplete, four feels excessive, three feels just right.
